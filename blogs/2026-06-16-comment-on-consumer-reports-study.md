Uber disputes Consumer Reports' findings about its pricing practices, arguing the study used a flawed methodology that treated trips with identical pickup and dropoff points as the "same" when changing real-time marketplace conditions (demand, driver availability, timing) made them genuinely different trips priced differently, not personalized. The company also challenges the report's conclusions about promotional discounts and take rates.
